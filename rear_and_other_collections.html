Rare and Other Collections

Explore the National Library's collection of rare books, manuscripts, official documents, and more.

Rare Books

The National Library has an impressive number of rare books and other reading materials.With the recommendations of the Review Committee, the present Rare Books Division started functioning as a separate division in December, 1973 under the charge of an Assistant Library & Information Officer after shifting of Rare Books Division to its new premises on Bhasha Bhavan Basement, National Library Kolkata from 1st February, 2013. At present the books published prior to 1860 are considered rare books, along with limited and first editions, books distinguished by their design, illustration or history, and a few other criteria. Along with rare books, manuscripts and microfilms of the library are also stacked in this division.

The division also provides reading facilities to the users who wish to consult these items. At present the division has 6,489 monographs, 6,623 manuscripts.

Science and Technology Collection

It is important to note that three committees in their reports “unanimously recommended the need of setting up of a well balanced science library in the National Library, Calcutta. The Science and Technology Division of the National Library were set up in April, 1972 in the ground floor of the Annexe Reading Room in accordance with the recommendation of the reviewing committee as well as of the Kamath committee. The committee of management headed by Prof. Niharranjan Ray as the Chairman was formed by the Government of India in October 1975 to advice the Department of Culture and the Acting librarian of the National Library regarding the functioning of the Library. This committee reviewed the acquisition policy of the of the library in the areas of science and technology and suggested that “since the National Library was not a specialized library, the library should not acquire highly specialized books in any branch of science and technology, which were being acquired by various other institutions but should concentrates on the following areas:

  • History of Science and Technology
  • Philosophy of Science
  • Science and Society
  • Science Culture and Civilization
  • Scientific Research Policy and Environmental Pollution
  • General Science Books and History of Invention

With accordance with the love for education and research, Science and Technology Division has been shifted from Annexe building to Bhasha Bhavan, 1st floor on 05/04/2010. Scientist, Engineers, Doctor, Entrepreneurs, Scholars, Students are the common readers of this division and they use reading room for study and research purpose. Eminent scholars who are not attached to any university or any research institute frequently visit this division for consultation of their research work. Science and Technology Division is the only place in the National Library which provides open access to its collection and users have freedom to brows its collection on the shelves directly of their research purpose. It maintains Author and Title catalogue and Subject catalogue, besides manual catalogue users can access online catalogue. Presently 17,000 reference books (approx) covering all subjects on Science and Technology and 400 titles of Indian and Foreign Science and Technological journals have been kept in the division for readers service. This division also provides internet services with Wi-Fi facility. Online journals and online books can be seen and use in the division. The division prepares bibliographies for the users and demand, makes searches for scientific and technical queries, replies IFLA and other organizational queries and organizes exhibitions etc.

Indian Official Documents

The collection of Indian Official documents division (IOD) of the National Library is, perhaps, the single largest most organized accumulation of government publications in the country. The collection is very rich and inclusive of both past and present. The collection ranges from the 18th Century from the days of the East India Company. It contains administrative reports, financial reports, reports on public health services, census reports, gazetteers, gazettes, legislative proceedings and so forth.

The division is located at the first floor of the Annexe Building and works as an independent composite unit since mid 70s. It is responsible for acquisition of government publications, their management, organisation and maintenance. The division receives official documents published by the Government of India, State Governments, Local Bodies, Legislatures, Public Undertakings, Banks, Government Institutions and Autonomous Bodies funded by the Central, State and Local Governments under the Delivery of Books Act. Limited readers’ service is also rendered by the division.

Machine readable cataloguing and automated serial control in MARC format are major achievements of the division in recent times. Bibliographic information of these documents is available in the website of the library. Few thousand old records of the collection have already been digitized and are available to the users with the library campus through intranet. Presently, the work of management and organizations of electronic Government Documents has been undertaken by the division.

Current collection:

  • Books, Journals, Gazettes: 166,839
  • Un-accessioned Materials: 50,000 (Approx)

Foreign Official Documents

The National Library is one of the repository libraries for United Nations Organisation and its agencies. Thus all the publications of UNO and its agencies are received by the library free of cost. These documents are processed and stacked separately. The library also receives publications of the governments of the United States of America, Great Britain, Canada, the Commonwealth nations, and the publications of the European Economic Committee. Almost all the volumes of the sessional sets of British Parliamentary papers since the beginning of the 19th century are available.

Current collection:

  • Accessioned Books: 199,204
  • Unprocessed Books & Periodicals: 300,000 (Approx)

Newspapers and Periodicals

(Click here for the list of Foreign Journals & Newspapers subscribed on renewal basis for the year 2015)

All the newspapers and periodicals in Indian languages are received and processed in their respective language divisions. But English newspapers and periodicals, both the Indian and foreign, are acquired and processed separately. A separate Serials division is responsible for acquiring and processing of English newspapers and periodicals. The library has a rich collection of late 19th and early 20th century newspapers and periodicals, but almost all of them are incomplete sets. There are catalogues for periodicals, newspapers and gazettes available in the library up to 1953.

Maps and Prints

This is the only full-fledged map library in the country with a very rich collection of old and rare maps and atlases. This division is located in the ground floor of the Annexe Building where it started functioning from mid 60s’ as an independent entity. The division is responsible for collection building of maps and atlases, their management and conservation. It renders services to the individuals as well as to the institutions. Cataloguing is done in machine readable format and is available in the website of the library.

Current collection:

  • Maps and Atlases: 55,000
  • Un-accessioned & Duplicate Maps: 33,000(Approx)

Microforms

The library has preserved valuable documents in microfilm and microfiche formats, including the Census of India (1872-1951).

Current collection:

  • Microfilms: 6,801 rolls
  • Microfiches: 18,422
