Indian Languages Collection

The National Library receives books and periodicals in almost all Indian languages under the Delivery of Books and Newspapers (Public Libraries) Act 1954 (D.B. Act).

Assamese Language Collection

In 1963, a separate division was established in the National Library to collect and process Assamese books. Currently, the division has 18,913 books, including rare works published between 1840 and 1900.

Bengali Language Collection

The library has more than 1 lakh books in its Bengali collection, including rare manuscripts, periodicals, and first editions of Rabindranath Tagore’s works.

Gujarati Language Collection

The Gujarati division, established in the sixties, now holds 44,793 books, including significant literary and religious texts.

Hindi Language Division

The Hindi division, established in 1960, has 1,66,544 books, including rare works from the late 18th century.

Kannada Language Collection

Formed in 1963, the Kannada division currently has around 53,000 books and includes rare works of Veerashaiva literature.

Kashmiri Language Collection

Established in 1983, the Kashmiri section contains significant literary works, but current acquisitions remain unprocessed due to staff shortages.

Malayalam Language Collection

With around 47,977 books, the Malayalam division has works dating back to 1791, including early grammar books and dictionaries.

Marathi Language Collection

Started in 1963, the Marathi division now has 55,588 books, including historical texts on the Marathas.

Oriya Language Collection

Established in 1973, the Oriya division contains 49,645 books, with publications dating back to 1831.

Punjabi Language Collection

Established in 1974, the Punjabi collection contains 23,729 books, including rare dictionaries and historical accounts.

Sanskrit, Pali, and Prakrit Languages Collection

The Sanskrit division has over 12,228 books, along with significant collections of Pali and Prakrit literature.

Sindhi Language Collection

Established in 1957, the Sindhi collection consists of 2,100 books, including rare literary works.

Tamil Language Collection

The Tamil division, formed in 1963, currently holds 1,12,847 books, including early printed Tamil books and manuscripts.

Telugu Language Collection

Established in 1963, the Telugu division holds 46,500 books, including rare 19th-century publications.

Urdu Language Collection

Formed in 1968, the Urdu division contains 30,000 books, including rare and historic manuscripts.
