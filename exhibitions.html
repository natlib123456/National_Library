Exhibition

Explore the stories of India's icons of nationalism and witness their inspiring legacy at the National Library.

Icons of Nationalism: From the Soil of Bengal

A Digital Exhibition on “Icons of Nationalism: from the Soil of Bengal” was inaugurated by Shri.Keshari Nath Tripathi,His Excellency, the Governor of West Bengal on 8th March,2019 at the renovated Belvedere house of the National Library. Shri.Satish Chandra Tewary IAS Addl.Chief Secretary to the Governor of West Bengal, Shri.Shravan Kumar, Director General, National Library, Dr.Rajesh Purohit, Director, Indian Museum were presented. The Family members of Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose also graced the occasion. A large number of intellectuals & Public assembled to witness the exhibition.

This Exhibition on “Icons of Nationalism: from the Soil of Bengal” was curated on the Lives & Times of Bankim Chandra Chattopadhyay, Rabindranath Tagore, Subhas Chandra Bose and Shyama Prasad Mookerjee. The edifice of free India was built on the sacrifices and martyrdom of thousands of patriots. While the flame of nationalism was ignited all over India, Bengal was one of the main theatres of India’s struggle for Freedom against British Imperialism. The soil of Bengal nurtured many nationalists whose sacrifices for the motherland live with us even today and the nation remains forever indebted to them. From the many nationalists that emerged from Bengal, certain personalities became the flag bearers for freedom, and thus ‘Icons of Nationalism’ Whether through their pen, paintings, music, or actions, they often charted a path for the independence movement. Their love for the motherland and strong nationalist ideologies rekindled the unified spirit of Indian nationalism.

This exhibition aims to enlighten the youth of the inspiring lifetimes of Bankim Chandra Chattopadhyay, Rabindranath Tagore, Subhas Chandra Bose and Shyama Prasad Mookerjee, and their unforgettable contribution to the freedom struggle.

This digital, interactive exhibition has been designed, developed and established by the National Library, Ministry of Culture, Government of India at the historic ‘Belvedere House’ of the National Library.

Along with this, The Indian Museum, Kolkata also curated an exhibition linking the past titled “Story of India through Images and Symbols” at the Belvedere House, National Library. The Exhibition is a collection of archeological artifacts of stone images depicting Buddha, Mahaveera, Vishnu, Shiva Shakti and symbols like Yantra, Lotus Feet of Buddha, Ashoka Chakra, etc.

Exhibition Highlights

This exhibition focuses on the lives and times of some of Bengal's most revered nationalists: Bankim Chandra Chattopadhyay, Rabindranath Tagore, Subhas Chandra Bose, and Shyama Prasad Mookerjee. These icons, through their writings, art, music, and actions, played pivotal roles in India's independence movement.

Bengal, as a major center of India's struggle for freedom, nurtured many nationalists whose sacrifices live on in the nation’s memory. Their contributions to the cause of Indian nationalism continue to inspire future generations.

Objective

This digital and interactive exhibition aims to enlighten the youth about the inspiring lives of these iconic figures and their unforgettable contributions to the freedom struggle. It has been designed and developed by the National Library, Ministry of Culture, Government of India, at the historic Belvedere House.

Additional Exhibition

Along with the primary exhibition, the Indian Museum, Kolkata, has curated an exhibition titled "Story of India through Images and Symbols." This collection showcases archaeological artifacts, including stone images of Buddha, Mahaveera, Vishnu, Shiva Shakti, and symbols like Yantras, the Lotus Feet of Buddha, and the Ashoka Chakra.
